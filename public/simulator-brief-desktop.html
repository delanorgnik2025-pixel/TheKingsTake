01 / Overview

What Is The AI Courtroom Simulator?

The AI Courtroom Simulator is a first-of-its-kind interactive educational platform that transforms anyone into a courtroom participant. Using advanced artificial intelligence, the simulator creates realistic legal proceedings complete with dynamic AI-powered personalities — judges, attorneys, witnesses, and jurors — each with distinct behavioral traits, biases, and courtroom styles.

Think of it as a "flight simulator for the legal system." Pilots train in simulators before flying real planes. Law students, pro se litigants, and curious citizens can now train in our simulator before facing a real courtroom.

⚖
Legal Positioning

This platform is explicitly designed and marketed as an educational game and simulation tool. It does not provide legal advice, create attorney-client relationships, or substitute for licensed legal counsel. All outcomes are generated by AI for learning purposes only.

📋
Game Classification Standards

The platform satisfies all criteria for educational game classification: (1) primary purpose is learning and entertainment, (2) no personalized legal recommendations, (3) outcomes do not affect real-world legal rights, (4) users do not rely on it as legal counsel, (5) all content is clearly labeled as simulated.

05 / Legal Compliance

UPL-Safe By Design

Unauthorized Practice of Law (UPL) statutes prohibit non-attorneys from providing legal advice or representing others in court. The AI Courtroom Simulator is architected from the ground up to remain firmly outside UPL boundaries.

✓ How We Stay Compliant
  • Educational Game Framework: Platform is marketed and operates as a simulation game, not a legal service.
  • No Legal Advice Given: All AI-generated content is labeled "for educational simulation purposes only."
  • No Attorney-Client Relationship: Users agree to Terms of Service explicitly disclaiming any attorney-client privilege or duty.
  • No Representation: The simulator does not file documents, appear in court, or communicate with real courts on behalf of users.
  • Prominent Disclaimers: Every session begins with a mandatory notice: "This is a simulation. Consult a licensed attorney for real legal matters."
  • Peer-Reviewed Content: All case templates and procedural rules are based on publicly available court rules, not tailored legal counsel.
  • No User Data Sold: All simulation inputs remain private and are never used to provide "legal guidance."
🎮
Why "Game" Classification Matters

By classifying as an educational game, the platform operates in the same legal category as flight simulators, medical procedure trainers, and historical war games. None of these constitute practicing medicine, flying planes, or waging war. Likewise, a courtroom simulation does not constitute practicing law.

🛡
First Amendment Protection

Educational content about legal procedures is protected speech. The simulator teaches how courts work — a constitutionally protected educational purpose. No state UPL statute has ever been successfully applied to educational simulation software.
